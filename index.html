Chevrolet Corvette

Americas Favorite Sports Car

The new seventh generation Corvette Stingray along with the previous six generations of Corvettes dating back to 1953.

Brief history of the Corvette:

  • 1953: The Corvette made it's debut in 1953. Only available in white, it featured a fiberglass body with an inline six cylinder engine producing about 150hp

  • 1963: The second generation Corvette was released in 1963, and the "Stingray" name was introduced. 1963 featured a unique split rear window design. These corvettes are commonly referred to as the "C2" generation or the "mid years"

  • 1968: The "C3" generation ran production until 1982. The legendary "Zr1" name was introduced in 1970

  • 1984: No Corvettes were produced for the 1983 year due to design changes and build quality issues. The fourth generation "C4" Corvette made its debut in 1984

  • 1997: The "C5" was a completely redesigned Corvette featuring a convertible, targa roof coupe, and fixed roof coupe. The Z06 name was used on 2001 to 2004 fixed roof coupes

  • 2005: The "C6" Corvette got rid of the old pop-up headlights. The Z06 model had the legendary 427 cubic inch engine making 505hp, and the Zr1 was brought back and supercharged making 638hp

  • 2014: The "Stingray" name was brought back for the redesigned "C7" generation. This current generation is styled exotically and has been a huge success. All models, including the Z06 and Zr1, are available as targa coupes and convertibles

The Corvette is an iconic and legendary sports car with lots of history and racing heritage
